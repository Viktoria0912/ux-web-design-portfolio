CareSphere

A digital health management platform that helps users reclaim valuable time and organize their health information efficiently.

My Role

Solo UX/UI Designer

Timeline

3 Months

Tools

Figma, AI, Midjourney

Project Overview

CareSphere isn't just a platform—it's a digital time machine that helps you reclaim valuable hours once wasted on managing appointments and searching for documents. In a world where health management has become increasingly complex, CareSphere offers a streamlined solution for organizing, accessing, and sharing health information for individuals and families.

The Problem

"USERS ARE FRUSTRATED BY CHAOTIC HEALTH MANAGEMENT SOLUTIONS"

We've all been there. Overwhelmed by scattered medical records and missed appointments. In the chaos of managing our health, we often resort to makeshift solutions that don't truly meet our needs. This leaves us frustrated and anxious about our well-being. It's time for a better way to manage our health!

Key Challenges Identified:

  • 70% of users report lost or misplaced health records
  • 40% struggle with record management across family members
  • 35% forget to schedule or attend follow-up appointments
  • 95% want an easy digital solution for health records

Research Process

I approached this project with a comprehensive research strategy to truly understand user pain points and needs in health management.

Research Aim

For my health app, I wanted to make life easier by getting rid of paper clutter, helping people manage their health, and making it simple for families to stay connected when it comes to medical stuff. My goal was to create something that actually solves real problems, not just another app people forget about.

Primary Research: Interviews

I wanted to hear about user's actual experiences, not just guess what their problems might be. To really get what people go through, I chose to talk to 3 different types of users:

  • Parents couple
  • Person taking care of an elderly relative
  • Young student dealing with a chronic condition

I researched them about following questions:

  • How people find important health documents quickly?
  • What makes managing family health so challenging?
  • Where do most people keep their medical records or scattered files?
  • When people need fast access to health info the most?
  • What situations make them wish for a better solution?

Thoughts from the interviews

"I can never find my kids' vaccination records when I need them."

"Health apps are too complicated—I just use paper."

"I don't need reminders, but I don't want another app syncing notifications."

"I wish I had all my medical documents in one place."

  2. Registration: Account creation & login
  3. Family Setup: Add family members profiles
  4. Permissions: Camera, storage, notifications

Core Features

The main functionality of CareSphere focuses on four key areas:

  • Document Scanner: Take photos of medical documents & make them searchable
  • Document Library: Organize & access all scanned documents by family members
  • Appointments: Schedule, track & receive reminders for medical appointments
  • Health Metrics: Track important health data for each family member

Wireframes

Based on user research and defined user flows, I created wireframes to establish the information architecture and layout:

Key Wireframing Decisions

  • Used a tab-based navigation for easy access to core features
  • Implemented a card-based interface for the dashboard to highlight key information
  • Created a step-by-step document scanning process with clear visual guidance
  • Designed family profile switching that's accessible throughout the app
  • Incorporated visual health metrics with easy-to-understand visualizations

Usability Testing

I conducted usability testing with 5 participants who fit our target user profile to evaluate the wireframe prototypes and identify areas for improvement.

Testing Metrics

  • Task Completion Rate: 87%
  • Time on Task: Average 1.2 minutes per key task
  • Error Rate: 12% (primarily in document organization)
  • User Satisfaction: 4.2/5

Key Findings

  • Users found the document scanning process intuitive
  • Family profile switching needed clearer visual indicators
  • Health metrics visualizations were well-received
  • Document organization needed a more flexible categorization system
  • Appointment reminder setup was confusing for some users

Iterations Based on Testing

Family Profile Switching

Added color coding and persistent avatar indicators to make it clearer which family member's profile is active

Document Organization

Implemented both category and tag-based organization with custom category creation

Appointment Reminders

Simplified the reminder setup process with preset options and clearer custom settings

Final Design

The final design for CareSphere focuses on three core principles:

LINK IT & SYNC IT

Connect profiles for your whole family, quickly share relevant health data, and easily monitor the health needs of children or elderly parents.

SCAN IT & FORGET IT

Take control of your health, foster an emotional connection with the app, and simplify the decision-making process by having all your important documents at your fingertips.

TRACK IT & HACK IT

Monitor your health metrics in real-time, track progress over time, and use smart reminders to keep you on track.

What I Learned

Research is Everything

Taking the time to thoroughly understand user pain points before jumping into design solutions was crucial. The interviews revealed nuances that wouldn't have been discovered through surveys alone.

Simplicity Wins

In health management apps, where users are already dealing with complexity, simplifying the interface and focusing on core functionality improved user satisfaction significantly.

Iterative Testing Pays Off

Testing early wireframes saved significant development time by identifying usability issues before they were built into the final design.

Emotional Design Matters

Health is personal and emotional. Incorporating design elements that build trust and reduce anxiety was as important as functional considerations.
